[1] Morris Dees is a co-founder and leader of the Southern Poverty Law Center, located in Montgomery, Alabama. [2] It was founded to battle racial bias and has expanded its efforts by tracking hate crimes and the increasing spread of racist organizations across the US. [3] "Teaching Tolerance" is a major program of the Center. [4] Under that program, a magazine promoting interracial and intercultural understanding goes to more than 400,000 teachers. [5] Other publications of the Center include the magazine "Intelligence Report" and pamphlets "Ten Ways to Fight Hate" and "Fighting Hate at School". [6] Dees has determined that the civil courts are an effective forum in which to attack and destroy hate groups. [7] He has used the civil lawsuit like a "Buck Knife, carving financial assets out of hate group leaders". [8] Some skeptics thought that Dees sought out victims of hate groups to profit from their tragedy. [9] However, Dees does not charge the groups and the Center estimates that it collects only 2% on successful judgments. [10] Dees has a perfect record in the major lawsuits he has prosecuted. [11] Successful judgments include one for $21.5M against a South Carolina branch of the Ku Klux Klan for burning the Macedonia Baptist Church. [12] Others include $6.3M against Aryan Nation's leader Richard Butler and $7M against a Klan group that killed a black man in Mobile, Alabama. [13] The Center operates mostly on contributions that in the late 1990s have increased to around $100 Million annually.
