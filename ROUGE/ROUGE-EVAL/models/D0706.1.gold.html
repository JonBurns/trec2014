[1] In 1990 Nobel Peace Prize winner Aung San Suu Kyi's National League for Democracy won an 82% parliamentary majority in Myanmar (formerly Burma). [2] Since then, the State Law and Order Restoration Council that seized power in 1988 and commits widespread ethnic cleansing and rape, has prevented Parliament from convening. [3] In January 1997 Suu Kyi was besieged in her van for 9 days by government thugs, to prevent her meeting elected lawmakers. [4] By September 1998 the SLORC had arrested 194 lawmakers-elect to prevent Parliament convening by or after NLD's Aug 21, 1998 deadline. [5] In 1998 the SLORC threatened 71-year old NLD Vice Chairman, Tin Oo, with re-imprisonment for continued political activities "violating" his parole. [6] In the World Trade Organization Europe and Japan challenged a US law banning government agencies from trading with anyone trading with Myanmar. [7] Myanmar banned British officials after being pressured about meeting with Suu Kyi. [8] The EU restricted arms and non-humanitarian aid to Myanmar. [9] The US and EU countries boycotted an international heroin conference in heroin rife Myanmar. [10] In 1999 17 armed groups "exchanged arms for peace" with Myanmar's military government. [11] Myanmar demanded Thailand control border refugee camps harboring foreign-sponsored military and "terrorist" training. [12] The Myanmar embassy in Bangkok was seized by Myanmar rebels. [13] In 2000 the US agreed to resettle 600 Burmese refugees. [14] The US denied anti-drug certification to Burma. [15] The Labor Department reported that Myanmar forces adult and child labor. [16] Suu Kyi received Dublin's "Freedom of the City" award in absentia, as did pop band U2.
