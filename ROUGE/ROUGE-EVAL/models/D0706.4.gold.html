[1] Aung San won Burma's independence from Britain in 1947 and was assassinated. [2] His daughter Suu Kyi headed the opposition, was harassed by the government and put under house arrest. [3] She wrote the famous essay "Freedom From Fear" and won the 1991 Nobel Peace Prize and Dublin's Freedom of the City honor in 2000. [4] Tin Oo, NLD vice-chairman, fought alongside Aung San and became a respected army officer. [5] He was imprisoned twice and placed under house arrest. [6] The military took over Burma in 1962. [7] The State Law and Order Restoration Council claimed power in 1988, massacring thousands and renaming Burma "Myanmar". [8] In 1990 the National League for Democracy (NLD) party won 82 percent of the vote but the military annulled the election. [9] The NLD set Aug 21, 1998 for convening the National Assembly. [10] The government made 900 political arrests that summer. [11] Refugees fled to Thailand. [12] The US accepted hundreds of Burmese student refugees. [13] Opposition guerrillas held the Myanmar embassy in Bangkok hostage for 25 hours in October 1999 and stormed a Thai hospital in January 2000. [14] Most foreign aid ended after 1988. [15] A 1996 Massachusetts law banning state contracts with anyone trading with Burma was overturned in court but other US localities passed similar laws. [16] The US and others banned Burmese trade and investment and boycotted a heroin conference in Myanmar. [17] Swedish telecommunications giant Ericsson ended Burmese operations when threatened with a boycott. [18] In 2000 the US denied anti-drug certification to Burma, a major heroin source. [19] AIDS is epidemic in Burma.
