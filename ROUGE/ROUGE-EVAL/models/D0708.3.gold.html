[1] Many countries of the world have recurring problems with shortages of potable water. [2] Many of the countries are in Africa and Asia. [3] In most cases, the water shortages are the result of weather conditions that lead to drought. [4] In poorer countries, where water sources have never been abundant and handling and distribution are not well developed, drought conditions are devastating. [5] Increasing demands for water overstress older, inefficient distribution systems in regions where supply is marginal and seasonally affected. [6] Northwest China is an area where chronic shortages of water demand new sources and a correction of past wasteful practices. [7] Bangladesh loses much water through mismanagement and is affected by weather conditions. [8] Water resources are scarce in Cyprus, Rwanda, Afghanistan, Ethiopia and Kenya and all suffer from seasonal drought. [9] The Philippines blame weather changes caused by El Nino and excessive usage for their water shortage. [10] Low river levels in Vietnam and Tanzania due to low rainfall caused their water shortage. [11] The Angolan capital of Luanda had a temporary water shortage when power was lost at the water plant. [12] South Africa needs a new management strategy to meet future needs. [13] Nepal has plenty of water but their system is poorly managed resulting in some shortages. [14] Lebanon's water shortages are due to old, leaky pipes and an inefficient distribution system. [15] Supply shortage is the main problem Iran, especially in Tehran. [16] Water shortages in Malaysia, Mexico, Thailand and Pakistan are due to prolonged drought conditions.
