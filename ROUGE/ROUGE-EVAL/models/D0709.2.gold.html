[1] Angelina Jolie's screen debut was in one of her father John Voight's movies "Looking to Get Out" (1982) at the age of 6. [2] As a young adult, her early movie roles include appearances in "Hackers" (1995) and "Foxfire" (1996). [3] She won back-to-back Golden Globe awards for her TV movie performances in "George Wallace" (1997) and "Gia" (1998). [4] Jolie took a short break from acting after "Gia" and studied film writing and directing at New York University. [5] She soon returned to acting and won Golden Globe, Screen Actors' Guild and Academy awards as best supporting actress in "Girl Interrupted" (1999). [6] Other film credits include "Playing God" (1998), "Playing by Heart" (1999), and "Pushing Tin" (1999). [7] She costarred with Denzel Washington in "The Bone Collector" (1999), the first film for which her name appeared above the title. [8] Soon after divorcing actor Jonny Miller, Jolie married "Pushing Tin" costar Billy Bob Thornton in a quickie Las Vegas ceremony in May 2000. [9] That same year, Jolie starred with Antonio Banderas in "Dancing in the Dark", and opposite Nicolas Cage and Delroy Lindo in "Gone in 60 Seconds", a box office hit despite poor reviews. [10] Aside from films, Jolie appeared on the spring 1999 cover of Allure and in the Rolling Stones video "Has Anybody Seen My Baby"? [11] Upcoming films credits will include starring as Lara Croft in "Tomb Raider" and with Kevin Costner and Meg Ryan in Oliver Stone's "Beyond Borders". [12] Jolie turned down a leading role in "Charlie's Angels".
