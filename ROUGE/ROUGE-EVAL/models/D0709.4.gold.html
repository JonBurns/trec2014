[1] Angelina Jolie trained at the Lee Strasberg Theater Institute. [2] Early roles in Hackers (1995) and Foxfire (1996) led to her Emmy and Golden Globe-winning role in the TNT miniseries "George Wallace" (1997). [3] She also acted in "Playing God" (1998), "Playing By Heart" (1999), and "The Bone Collector", and walked down a NY street in her underwear in a Rolling Stones video. [4] She was to star in a remake of "Charlie's Angels" but turned it down for "Gone in 60 Seconds", about car thief gangs. [5] Her role in the HBO movie "Gia" won her Golden Globe and Emmy awards in 1999. [6] After her acceptance speech she dove into the hotel swimming pool in her beaded Randolph Duke dress. [7] After "Gia" she needed a break and took writing and directing classes at NY University, coming back with a rediscovered love for acting. [8] For "Girl, Interrupted" (1999) she won the Golden Globe and Screen Actors Guild awards, and an Oscar for Best Supporting Actress. [9] In her Oscar acceptance speech, a gushing reference to her brother Jaime Haven led to rumors hinting at incest, which she denied. [10] In 2000 her upcoming movies included "Tomb Raider", "Beyond Borders", and "Dancing in the Dark". [11] She was married briefly to "Trainspotting" star Jonny Lee Miller, whom she met while making "Hackers". [12] In May 2000, 24 year-old Jolie became the fifth wife of 44 year-old Billy Bob Thornton, whom she met on the set of "Pushing Tin" (1999). [13] She got a 10th tattoo, "Billy Bob", on her shoulder.
