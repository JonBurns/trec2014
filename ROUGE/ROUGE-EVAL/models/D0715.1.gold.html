[1] At least 138 countries signed the Ottawa Convention on Anti-Personnel Mines. [2] Among them were: South Africa, Namibia, Angola, Senegal, Sudan and 33 other African countries including the 14 Southern African Development Community states and 45 of the 53 Organization of African Unity states; Jordan, France, Germany, Spain, Britain, Cambodia, Hungary, Thailand, Guinea-Bissau, and inferably Canada, Austria, Norway and most other European states, Mozambique and 6 other Community of Portuguese states. [3] The treaty was ratified by 101 or more countries. [4] Ukraine refused because it can't afford to destroy landmines. [5] It stopped manufacturing mines, suspended mine exports and may sign in future. [6] The United States refused because it uses landmines in Korea and wants to exempt anti-personnel mines that protect non-treaty anti-tank mines. [7] Russia and China refused, claiming landmines are needed for defensive purposes. [8] Lebanon refused because of Israeli occupation. [9] Other non-signers were: Yugoslavia, Congo, most middle-east and many Asian nations and inferably Kosovo, Chechnya, Myanmar, India, Pakistan, Turkey, Columbia, Sri Lanka, and Afghanistan. [10] Mozambique urged 50 non-signing Community of Portuguese states to join the treaty. [11] Signers have destroyed over 22 million landmines. [12] Spain destroyed over 800,000 landmines. [13] About 12 countries destroyed all their landmines. [14] Russia, the U.S. and other non-signers pledged to stop manufacturing and exporting landmines. [15] Landmine trade almost completely halted. [16] Britain eliminated landmine ban exceptions for "exceptional circumstances". [17] The U.S. set 2006 to sign the treaty. [18] The treaty is undermined by U.S. lobbying for NATO countries to stockpile mines until 2003. [19] Landmine use continues in some signing countries.
