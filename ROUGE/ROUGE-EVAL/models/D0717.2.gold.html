[1] American Home Products Corp. removed the diet drug fenfluramine, also known as Pondimin or the fen in fen-phen, from the market in 1997 after a study linked it to potentially fatal heart valve injury. [2] In Texas, a number of plaintiffs sued the manufacturer for injuries resulting from fenfluramine use. [3] Sandra Moore sued for her mild heart valve damage and settled for a reported $500,000. [4] Debbie Lovett sued for her moderate to severe damage. [5] She eventually settled for a fraction of the $23.3 million a jury awarded her. [6] Survivors of Mary Smith alleged she died of primary pulmonary hypertension resulting from taking fenfluramine. [7] The trial ended with a $6-$7 million settlement. [8] American Home Products reportedly paid over $3 million to 70-year-old Ruth Burks, who attributed her rare lung disease to the drug. [9] Former fenfluramine user Virginia Brinkley, who was facing a third valve-replacement surgery, settled her lawsuit for $8-9 million. [10] In Mississippi, the company agreed to pay $350-400 million to settle 1,400 injury cases after a jury awarded $150 million to five other plaintiffs. [11] Four weeks later, six more women sued there. [12] Several wrongful death cases in the country were settled for about $4-5 million each. [13] Survivors of Mary Linnen of Massachusetts settled for an undisclosed amount. [14] Over 4,100 lawsuits have been filed against American Home Products over fenfluramine. [15] In October 2000, the company settled a class-action suit, agreeing to pay a $3.75 billion to settle thousands of lawsuits filed nationwide. [16] The deal would pay individual plaintiffs up to $1.5 million.
