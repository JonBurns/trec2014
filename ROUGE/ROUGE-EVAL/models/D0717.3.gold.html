[1] During 1999 and 2000, over 6100 lawsuits nationwide were filed, suing American Home Products Corp. [2] (AHPC) on the issue of serious heart problems caused by its diet-drug fen-Phen (a combination of fenfluramine and Phentermine) taken by over six million people. [3] Fenfluremine is marketed by Wyeth-Ayerst as Pondimin or dexfenfluramine, a similar drug sold by the company, as Redux. [4] In the first lawsuit, a women suffering from heart valve damage sued for $110 million but settled for $500,000. [5] In May 1999, a Texas women sued AHPC for her heart valve damage after taking fen-Phen for eight months. [6] She reportedly settled for less than 10% of the $23.3 million awarded by a jury. [7] At that time, AHPC reported that more than 2,600 lawsuits over the health effects of fen-Phen were pending. [8] A national settlement with AHPC was being discussed but it was only in preliminary stages. [9] The first full trial of a lawsuit involving the death of a fen-Phen patient occurred in Texas in May 1999. [10] At the time, lawyers said several fen-Phen deaths have been settled for about $4 -$5 million each. [11] Another Texas patient received $8-$9 million, the largest settlement to date for an individual. [12] Collective settlements were made to groups of citizens in New Jersey, Massachusetts, Texas and Mississippi in efforts to settle the lawsuits nationwide. [13] In August 2000, a federal judge approved a $3.75 billion national AHPC settlement of health claims against fen-Phen. [14] Users would receive up to $1.5 million for treatment and for future medical monitoring.
