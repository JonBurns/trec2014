[1] Starbucks Coffee has expanded and diversified its business in a number of ways. [2] In joint ventures with basketball legend Magic Johnson, Starbucks opened shops in the inner-city neighborhoods of Seattle's Columbia City, West Lost Angeles and New York's Harlem. [3] It joined with Time Inc. to publish Joe magazine, to be sold exclusively in Starbucks coffeehouses. [4] With The New York Times it agreed to sell the newspapers in American stores for three years in exchange for advertising promotion. [5] Starbucks teamed with online urban delivery service Kozmo.com for cross-marketing and in-store promotion. [6] Kozmo drop-boxes will be in Starbucks wherever the service operates. [7] Starbucks acquired San Francisco-based Pasqua Coffee, adding 56 outlets to its network. [8] It acquired high-quality tea maker Tazo, giving it a foothold in the specialty tea market. [9] It established a European beachhead by acquiring the Seattle Coffee Co. in the UK. [10] Starbucks purchased upscale music retailer Hear Music to continue its tradition of selling music CDs in its shops. [11] A rumored takeover offer for Williams-Sonoma Inc. was unsuccessful. [12] Starbucks created a consumer products division for its specialty items. [13] Most of them have been joint ventures: ice cream with Dreyer's Grand Ice Cream; bottled Frappuccino with Pepsi-Cola; and the supermarket distribution of its coffee with Kraft Foods. [14] It also announced a line of premium chocolates. [15] Starbucks will form a new subsidiary, Starbucks X, to direct its expansion into online sales with a web site that will sell gourmet food, kitchen products and home furnishings as well as coffee products and music.
