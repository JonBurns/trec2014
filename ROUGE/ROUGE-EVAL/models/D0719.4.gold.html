[1] During the latter part of the 1990s, France along with several other European countries experienced high rates of unemployment. [2] In the early part of this period, the unemployment rate for persons of working age reached 12.5%. [3] France's economy during this period was very sluggish with the growth rate near zero. [4] Structural problems and lack of flexibility within the economy have led to the high unemployment rate. [5] High public spending is a factor as are high labor costs, especially for unskilled labor. [6] Conditions did improve and France closed out the decade with its unemployment rate lowered to 10.6%. [7] The high unemployment rate led to protests and demonstrations by workers and included some violence. [8] Social disparity heightened, crime rates rose as did the rate of suicides. [9] The government did increase unemployment benefits and in some cases provide holiday bonuses to alleviate hardship caused by the situation. [10] One proposal was made to cut working hours for workers and then hire more employees but this generated little hope of success. [11] In the long term, France should concentrate on achieving wage flexibility and reducing the cost of unskilled labor.
