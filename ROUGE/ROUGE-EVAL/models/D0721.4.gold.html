[1] Matthew Shepard, a 21-year old University of Wyoming student was savagely bludgeoned and left tied to a fence in freezing weather. [2] His skull too crushed for surgery. [3] He died four days later. [4] Aaron McKinney and Russell Henderson were tried and convicted of Shepard's kidnapping and murder. [5] Each was sentenced to two consecutive life terms. [6] The crime occurred at a time when some conservatives and groups were demonizing gays and lesbians. [7] The extreme brutality of the crime aroused the gay community and candlelight vigils and demonstrations were held at several locations across the nation. [8] On the first anniversary of the attack, 600 people held a vigil in Laramie and a play about the crime, "The Laramie Project", was presented in Denver. [9] Shepard's murder, in part, prompted over 450 bills to be introduced in state legislatures on issues important to gays and lesbians. [10] Senator Kennedy introduced a bill to expand the 1968 Hate Crimes legislation to include offenses motivated by sexual orientation, sex or disability. [11] Aaron McKinney, who actually delivered the blows to Shepard's head that eventually killed him, stood trial. [12] Henderson had earlier pled guilty. [13] Fearful of the death penalty, McKinney's attorneys admitted manslaughter. [14] The trial judge disallowed their use of the "gay panic" defense, which theorizes that a person with latent homosexual tendencies would react violently if propositioned by a gay. [15] McKinney was presented as one who had been sexual abused which led him to drug addiction and alcohol abuse.
