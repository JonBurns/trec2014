[1] Dianne Feinstein was elected to the U.S. Senate in 1992 after an unsuccessful run for Governor of California in 1990. [2] She defeated Gov. Pete Wilson's hand-picked successor, receiving more votes than any Senate candidate in American history. [3] She had to run again in 1994 since she was finishing Wilson's incomplete term, and in that election she edged Rep. Huffington, who spent $30 million on the race, by 163,000 votes. [4] Feinstein faced reelection again in 2000 by which time her political and legislative accomplishments made her a clear favorite with polls showing her lead over Rep. Tom Campbell among decided voters as 57%-34%. [5] While serving in the Senate 1993-2000 Feinstein established a solid record as a seasoned politician and one of the best speakers in current American politics. [6] Some regard her as a centrist able to work with members of both parties, and others as generally-but not uniformly-a liberal. [7] She became the top Democrat on the Senate Judiciary Committee's panel on technology and gained a seat on the powerful Appropriations Committee. [8] Sen. Feinstein was the first Senate Democrat to speak out publicly condemning Pres. [9] Clinton's lying to the American people and she sponsored an unsuccessful censure resolution. [10] The crowning glories of her Senate career were the creation of a new national park in the Mojave Desert and enactment of a ban on assault weapons (1994). [11] As the Democratic National Convention approached in 2000 there was much speculation that Dianne Feinstein might be the Vice Presidential nominee.
