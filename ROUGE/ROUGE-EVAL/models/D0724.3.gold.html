[1] Obesity is defined as being 20% heavier than your ideal weight. [2] Obesity in the U.S., the fattest nation on earth, is an epidemic costing the healthcare system 50 billion dollars and causing 300,000 deaths annually. [3] Over half of Americans are overweight. [4] One third of American adults are obese. [5] At the current rate, everyone in America will be obese by 2030. [6] A 1999 survey in Los Angeles county found that 55% of men and 39% of women are overweight; African-Americans have the highest rate of obesity at 58%, Latinos 44%, whites 44% and Asian-Americans the lowest, 25%. [7] The number of overweight Californians jumped 50% between 1984 and 1997. [8] Since 1991, obesity in the southeast jumped 67.2% with Georgia leading at 101.8%. [9] Southerners are less likely to exercise or join a health club. [10] The lowest obesity rates were in New England and in the West. [11] The reasons for U.S. obesity are in our culture of abundance and inactivity. [12] A primary factor is consuming more calories than you burn off. [13] A root cause is a sedentary lifestyle combined with readily available high-calorie and fat-rich food. [14] Getting food was hard work 100 years ago whereas it is easy today without requiring physical activity. [15] Social and work environments encourage overeating; restaurants compete by offering bigger servings; technology contributes to avoiding exercise. [16] Geneticists have found that heredity also plays a role and there is evidence that obesity is linked to hormonal irregularities. [17] A 1995 study in England linked overeating with pathological behavior.
