[1] On July 29, 1999 Newt Gingrich filed for divorce from his wife of 18 years, Marianne, declaring the marriage "irretrievably broken". [2] Marianne filed a counterclaim asking a judge to freeze the couple's assets accusing Newt of transferring assets before filing for divorce. [3] Newt denied the charge but a confidential financial agreement pending a divorce settlement was reached on Sept. 10. [4] Marianne requested and a judge granted a videotaped deposition of a 33-year-old Congressional aide, Callista Bisek, with whom Newt was alleged to have had an affair. [5] Bisek appealed to another judge to overrule the decision. [6] Marianne then asked permission to depose Newt's close advisors and associates to get answers to questions that he ignored. [7] Newt responded by calling on Marianne to sit for a videotaped deposition and to account for all the couple's cash and stocks, suggesting that she had evaded their confidential financial agreement. [8] The agreement was subsequently released to the public. [9] After a judge ruled that Bisek must testify, Newt volunteered that he would question her. [10] Bisek admitted their six-year affair, but refused to comply when Marianne's attorney subpoenaed various gifts, letters and documents. [11] Outstanding legal issues were never settled since after going to mandatory mediation, the Gingriches reached "full settlement" on Dec. 16. [12] Final signing of the settlement on April 3, 2000 asked that the judge seal the agreement so that its terms would remain secret. [13] One aspect of the outcome was announced, however: Newt and Callista Bisek would be married August 18, 2000.
