[1] In May 1999 Newt Gingrich told his wife he was seeing another woman and wanted a divorce. [2] He separated from his wife in May and filed for divorce in July. [3] Marianne accused him of transferring assets before seeking a divorce. [4] She sought to depose Callista Bisek, Newt's alleged mistress, and four of Newt's business/political associates. [5] Newt countered with an attempt to blame his wife for the marriage collapse, demanding she be deposed and accusing her of withholding money. [6] Their accountant stated that both Newt and his wife acted in good faith. [7] The couple agreed to rely on a financial agreement drawn up during a previous separation and Newt asked the judge to settle the divorce based on that agreement. [8] In order to keep their private lives out of public view the couple reached an agreement on how to divide finances during the separation. [9] In December 1999 a court-ordered mediation was held. [10] Newt was prepared to split his assets and pay alimony. [11] After 12 hours of negotiation the couple agreed to a settlement. [12] Three months later the divorce was final and the settlement was signed. [13] Callista Bizek was not deposed but agreed to reveal some, but not all, of Newt's gifts and turned over some documents. [14] None of Newt's four political/business associates was deposed. [15] Newt and Callista were married in August 2000.
