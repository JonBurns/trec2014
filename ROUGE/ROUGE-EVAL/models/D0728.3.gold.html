[1] A genetic engineering drug, interferon, has been developed from protein isolated from human cells. [2] It is proving effective in the treatment of liver diseases hepatitis C and hepatitis B, multiple sclerosis, and life-threatening tumors. [3] It is in the early stages of study in the treatment of cancer with some promising results. [4] Until recently, it was the only treatment for hepatitis but now there is a new treatment approach involving combinations of interferon and ribavirin. [5] It is manufactured as Rebetron by Schering-Plough Corp. and is sold as a kit. [6] The interferon is sold as Intron A, the only type approved for use with ribavirin. [7] Other forms are increasingly available from different manufacturers. [8] Interferon alone eliminates hepatitis C in about 10-20 percent of patients, about 50 percent do not respond, while the remaining 30-40 percent respond and then relapse. [9] In the combination theory, after relapse the patient is given the ribavirin. [10] Studies show that after six months all signs of the virus were gone in 82 percent of those on the combination compared with half on interferon alone. [11] Interferon can cause serious side effects such as fever, lethargy, clogged saliva glands, chronic yeast infection, urinary tract infections, and depression. [12] The drug combination can cause breathing difficulty, sore throat, rashes, itching, nausea, insomnia, anemia, anorexia and occasionally birth defects. [13] Schering-Plough's treat kit costs about $1,831 for a six-week supply. [14] A six-month treatment costs $6,400 to $8,000. [15] Patients accuse Schering of "bundling" the products in order to compel them to use its interferon.
