[1] Eric Robert Rudolph was charged with bombing an abortion clinic in Birmingham, AL on Jan. 29, 1998 and three earlier bombings in Atlanta, GA 1996-97. [2] The Birmingham bombing killed one person and injured another. [3] The Olympic Park bombing in Atlanta killed one and injured 111 while bombings at an Atlanta abortion clinic and a gay bar injured nine. [4] Pursuit of Rudolph began within hours of the Birmingham bombing. [5] The operation involved the FBI, the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms and local legal authorities. [6] The search scoured the Nantahala National Forest in North Carolina where Rudolph lived as a boy. [7] The area is heavily forested mountainous terrain with peaks reaching to 5000 feet, heavy hardwood forests and thickets of virtually impenetrable rhododendron. [8] Hundreds of mines and caves dot the area. [9] It was thought that Rudolph, an accomplished sportsman and survivalist, was able to hide in inaccessible mines or caves while stealing food for sustenance. [10] The search for Rudolph involved as many as 200 armed officers and employed bloodhounds and helicopters with infrared viewing capability. [11] The FBI placed Rudolph on its ten most wanted list and offered a reward of $1 million for information leading to his apprehension. [12] After a year without success the search was scaled back and in March 2002 it was announced that the task force would dismantle its command post in June. [13] In August it was reported that the 31-month unsuccessful pursuit of Rudolph had cost $24.6 million.
