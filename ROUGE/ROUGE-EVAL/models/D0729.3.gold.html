[1] Eric Rudolph, a 31-year old carpenter is wanted for bombing an Alabama abortion clinic on 29 January 1998 and three bombings in the Atlanta area in 1996 and 1997. [2] An explosion July 27, 1996 at Centennial Olympic Park killed one woman and injured 111 people. [3] In a pair of explosions January 27, 1997, seven people were injured outside an Atlanta abortion clinic. [4] A bombing at a gay nightclub in Atlanta February 21, 1997 injured five people. [5] Rudolph became a suspect when witnesses saw a man acting suspiciously shortly after the January 1998 bombing. [6] A North Carolina license plate on the man's truck was traced to him. [7] Six months later, Rudolph was identified by a food store owner near Nantahale Lake in Western North Carolina not far from where he first lived. [8] He stole the man's truck and took about a six-month supply of food. [9] About 200 FBI, state, and local officials began searching the remote densely forested area employing helicopters, dogs, and survival-training volunteers. [10] Evidence from the abandoned truck, buried trash, and boot prints proved he was in the area. [11] A one-million dollar reward for information, distribution of photos, and prime-time TV exposure were employed to no avail. [12] Rudolph has eluded police for three years. [13] He is an experienced survivalist, knows the area well, has altered his identity by growing a beard and long hair, and is probably hiding in one of the area's hundreds of caves or mines. [14] The search effort has been reduced to relying on volunteer information.
