[1] Eric Rudolph was charged in several bombings, including three in Atlanta, Georgia (Centennial Olympic Park, an abortion clinic in Atlanta, and a gay nightclub), and one at a Birmingham, Alabama, abortion clinic. [2] Rudolph was connected to the Alabama bombing but escaped a police dragnet and, loaded up with food and supplies, hid for five and a half months in the woods of western North Carolina. [3] He emerged only once to obtain a six-month supply of food and steal a pick-up - then disappeared into the woods again. [4] He was known to be an outdoorsman and survivalist and was hiding in familiar woods near his childhood home. [5] The man from whom Rudolph had obtained the food and truck had waited at least two days to report the stolen pickup, but was not suspected of aiding Rudolph. [6] He provided an updated description of him, thinner with beard and ponytail. [7] In searching for Rudolph the government conducted one of the most extensive manhunts in recent history. [8] The FBI put him on the 10-Most-Wanted list and offered a million dollar reward for information leading to his arrest. [9] As many as 200 agents at a time were searching the woods for him. [10] Investigators used trackers, helicopters, dogs, and sophisticated surveillance equipment. [11] In March 2000, the task force was to dismantle the command post because the trail had grown cold. [12] The publicly acknowledged cost of the 31-month search was 24.6 million dollars. [13] As of 18 August 2000 Rudolph had not been found.
