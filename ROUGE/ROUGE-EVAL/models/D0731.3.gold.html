[1] Linda Tripp was a key player in the Ken Starr criminal investigation of President Bill Clinton. [2] Tape recordings that Tripp secretly made of her conversations with White House intern Monica Lewinsky triggered that investigation. [3] Linda Tripp was an older woman with whom Monica Lewinsky became a close friend. [4] Lewinsky confided the most intimate details of an affair she was having with a married man. [5] They often had as many as 20 phone calls or emails a day. [6] It was Tripp who recommended that Lewinsky hang on to the blue dress stained from an encounter with the president. [7] Tripp delivered over 20 hours of taped conversation she had with Lewinsky to the Ken Starr investigation. [8] Tripp underwent three days of testimony before the grand jury. [9] When she emerged from her final day of testimony, she tried to portray herself as a victim rather than a schemer. [10] She said "I'm just like you". [11] Although Tripp had a New York Book agent, she claimed that she was not writing a book about Clinton and Lewinsky. [12] Tripp recorded her phone calls with Lewinsky in violation of Maryland law. [13] Tripp had been granted immunity by Starr so her tapes could not be used in Maryland court. [14] There was one tape that Lewinsky, in testimony, believed pre-dated the grant of immunity but it could not be confirmed so Tripp did not face trial. [15] Tripp did incur legal costs of over $325,000.
