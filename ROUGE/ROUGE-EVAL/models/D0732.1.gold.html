[1] The Kenyan government is taking several steps to raise the educational level of its populace. [2] One proposal aimed at improving the economic climate in the country is the introduction of Entrepreneurship Education in primary schools in order to inculcate a business and enterprising culture in the minds of learners at an early age. [3] The country also aims to improve educational opportunities for women, furnish environmental conservation education and has asked the international community for aid in implementing HIV/AIDS awareness in schools. [4] Kenya's Ministry of East African and Regional Cooperation has called for the harmonization of education in the east African region. [5] This move would help attain a state of singularity in the region's economic management and social organization. [6] Kenya's 8-4-4 education system (eight years primary and four each of high school and university) is different from Uganda and Tanzania making regional integration difficult. [7] Strengthening Primary Education (SPRED) is a project jointly developed by the Kenyan and British governments and funded by the Britain's Overseas Development Authority. [8] The World Bank has made a $26M grant to Kenya to help develop early childhood education. [9] The United States will give $350,000 to a Kenyan project aimed at linking the east African universities to the Internet within a year and 600 primary and secondary schools within three years. [10] Kenya will allow foreign universities to establish constituent campuses in that country.
