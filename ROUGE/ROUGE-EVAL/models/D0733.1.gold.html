[1] The Library houses numerous special book collections such as 7700 precious Tibetan books, including a gift from the Dalai Lama, and it is a repository for personal papers of historical interest such as the Private papers of Martin Luther King. [2] The Library features exhibits in its facilities such as the extensive exhibit on the life and work of Sigmund Freud. [3] It also sponsors traveling Literary exhibits such as an exhibit providing a historical survey of religion and the founding of the United States. [4] The Library has a resident Poet Laureate who presides at readings where prominent and ordinary people read their favorite poems. [5] In 1990 the Library began duplicating much of its holdings in electronic form, increasing access to the material. [6] In October 1998 Alexa Internet donated to the Library 44 computer disks containing a snapshot of the contents of the World Wide Web. [7] This formed the basis for exploratory work by the Library on how to preserve digital materials, resolve copyright issues, and provide access to the materials. [8] The Library set up an American Memory website on the Internet containing hundreds of letters. [9] It hoped to have 4 million items digitized and accessible on the Internet by 2000. [10] The Library developed Eyes of the Nation, a CD-ROM exploring the American experience. [11] It includes rare books, photos, numerous images and multimedia exhibits, and links to its website, a novel resource for teaching history. [12] A program called American Memory Fellows shows teachers how to use its collection in the classrooms.
