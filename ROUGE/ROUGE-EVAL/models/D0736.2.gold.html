[1] Oprah Winfrey has become the richest and most influential woman in entertainment. [2] Her popularity stems primarily from her TV talk show that has won 32 daytime Emmy's in its focus on family relationships, health, fashion, beauty, and books. [3] Oprah started a book club to encourage inspirational reading and has invited writers such as Robert Morgan, author of "Gap Creek", and change-your-life guru, John Gray, to discuss their books. [4] She also, for the first time, recently invited presidential candidate Al Gore, followed a week later by George Bush, both in an appeal for women's votes. [5] Recently she has begun featuring a "change your life" theme on her show that focuses on who you are and a truth-will-set-you-free attitude. [6] Much of her show's subject matter stems from her own sexually abused girlhood. [7] She is developing a new TV channel named Oxygen and starting a magazine, both intended to inspire women. [8] Her book club promotions have won her a 50th Anniversary Medal by the National Book Foundation. [9] She has produced TV movies based on some of the promoted books. [10] Her show declined, however, briefly after her disappointing role in "Beloved". [11] She also faced a lawsuit because her show allegedly "melodramatized" the mad cow disease. [12] She was cleared of giving false information and defaming cattle producers. [13] Oprah received high praises for teaching a class at Northwestern University on "Dynamics of Leadership". [14] The TV movie "Oprah Winfrey Presents: Tuesdays with Morrie" was named best TV movie in September 2000. [15] She still inspires promotional reading.
