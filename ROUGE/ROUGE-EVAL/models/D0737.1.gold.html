[1] The ocean covers about 70 percent of the Earth's surface, but 90 percent of the space remains unknown to humans. [2] A variety of robots, submersibles, and research vessels are being used to conduct deepwater exploration and research to close this gap in understanding. [3] Also, special methods are being used for deepwater oil and mineral exploration. [4] Much has been learned from these endeavors. [5] It has been found that animals living on the ocean floor may be as susceptible to climate changes as surface dwellers. [6] A rock sample recovered from deep beneath the Antarctic could reveal what happened to the continent millions of years ago. [7] Evidence has been found showing that petroleum exploration and rapid economic development have caused serious environmental pollution and damage to marine ecology. [8] Ancient Roman shipwrecks, Egyptian artifacts, and Byzantine coins have been found in the Mediterranean. [9] Cracks in the seabed were found off Virginia and North Carolina which could set off a tsunami. [10] There is a broad range of planned deepwater activities. [11] A new submersible will study deepwater ecosystems and the huge slabs of crust constantly being created and destroyed. [12] The depths will be searched for organisms that could provide medical cures. [13] New exploration projects are planned for deepwater canyons off mid-Atlantic states, for reefs beneath the Gulf of Mexico, and for an ocean mountain off California. [14] A global initiative is underway to determine how the oceans tick and influence climate change. [15] Efforts will continue to locate ancient harbors and to visit sites of sunken ships.
