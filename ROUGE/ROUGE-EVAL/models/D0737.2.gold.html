[1] Deep-sea explorations worldwide have led to historic discoveries. [2] US teams discovered five Roman shipwrecks during a Mediterranean exploration and two deep-water sea wrecks off the coast of Israel. [3] Divers discovered pharaonic statues, sphinxes, and Byzantine coins off Egypt's Mediterranean coast. [4] One exploration off the New York coast searched for signs of human settlements predating land ones. [5] The Chinese built a robot to survey ocean depths of 6,000 meters. [6] They have also made advances in the exploration of seabed polymetallic nodules. [7] China collected natural benthal electric data using an electromagnetic survey machine at 200 meters under the sea. [8] The country planted seabed forests to restore damage to its coastline from marine development, offshore petroleum exploration and industrial development. [9] Through deep-sea exploration scientists have learned about the ocean environment. [10] Scientists recovered the first rock sample from under the Antarctic Ocean bed. [11] They found that animals living on the ocean floor seem as susceptible to climate as surface dwellers. [12] They found oscillations involving deep ocean currents that appear similar to El Nino and La Nina. [13] They discovered cracks in the seabed that might set off a tsunami, threatening Mid-Atlantic States. [14] Dutch scientists found toxic chemicals in whales that feed in the Atlantic Ocean. [15] US scientists have improved craft and submersibles used for sea explorations in response to international competition. [16] In 2000 President Clinton announced federal participation in new undersea exploration projects on the East, West, and Gulf coasts and called for recommendations for new ocean exploration from a Commerce Department advisory panel.
