[1] Deep water exploration has revealed the Black Sea's history as a shallower freshwater lake, Roman shipwrecks spanning centuries, well-preserved Phoenician ships, ancient deep-water trade routes, sunken cities and vessels, rich ecosystems, new life species, geological formations, and the Titanic. [2] Wished-for exploration would seek millions in lost jewels and ice age human settlements off NY. [3] Scientists have drilled holes in seabeds to retrieve sediment and rock for analysis to understand the earth's past. [4] Ostracod fossils have demonstrated that seafloor creatures are affected by climate change. [5] Compounds found in deep-feeding whale tissue suggests industrial chemicals are not fully destroyed by incineration. [6] A deep ocean current oscillation has been discovered that warms and cools global areas over centuries or millenniums. [7] Seabed cracks off VA and NC that could set off tsunamis are being tested for geologic activity. [8] Other undersea exploration could produce organisms providing medical cures or lessons for industry and human development. [9] Seismic studies and drilling have located deep water oil reserves. [10] Other discoveries of seabed resources include benthal, natural gas-hydrate, and scattered polymetallic lumps that can be gathered by deep-water robot. [11] China pioneered using cylindrical steel caissons as oil drilling platforms and is planting seaweed forests to combat pollution and exploration damage. [12] Deep water exploration has been advanced by new submersible robotic vehicles that can operate at depths up to 4 miles and are precisely controlled. [13] Other aids are electronic cameras, high-resolution sonar, deep-water tracking equipment, and a global positioning satellite. [14] In future, robots could lay seabed detectors to track seaquakes.
