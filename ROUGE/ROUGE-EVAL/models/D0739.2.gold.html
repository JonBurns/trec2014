[1] The "Seinfeld" series on NBC was one of the most popular TV shows of the 1990s. [2] It was created by Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David, and written largely by Larry David. [3] In addition to Jerry Seinfeld playing himself, the show featured friends of Jerry. [4] The main roles were played by Jason Alexander as George Costanza, Julia Louis-Dreyfus as Elaine Benes, and Michael Richards as Cosmo Cramer. [5] Supporting actors on the show included Wayne Knight as Newman, Phil Morris as a lawyer, and Patrick Warburton as Puddy. [6] After the show stopped running in mid-1998, Jerry Seinfeld briefly returned to stand-up comedy. [7] He appeared in a 10-run Broadway show, "I'm Telling You For the Last Time," that culminated in a live HBO special. [8] Jason Alexander appeared in 2000 as Boris Badenov in the movie version of "Rocky and Bullwinkle". [9] Michael Richards appeared in 2000 on "The Michael Richards Show" playing a befuddled detective. [10] Many of the actors who played supporting roles on "Seinfeld," turned to advertising: Knight appeared in ads for a drug; Childs, in soft drink and car ads; and Warburton, in car and candy ads. [11] Larry David appeared in a new HBO series "Curb Your Enthusiasm" playing himself. [12] Viewers missed "Seinfeld". [13] Without "Seinfeld," NBC's ratings fell sharply, with the biggest loss being young male viewers. [14] NBC struggled to recover these viewers. [15] A web-site began offering free pills to ease depression caused being unable to watch "Seinfeld". [16] In a $100 million cable deal, TBS purchased rights to syndicate reruns of "Seinfeld".
