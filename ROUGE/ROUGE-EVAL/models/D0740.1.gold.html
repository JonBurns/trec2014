[1] Swiss balloonist Bertand Piccard was forced to abandon his first attempt to circumnavigate the world non-stop in a balloon six hours after take-off because of a fuel leak, January 12, 1997. [2] His second attempt began January 28, 1998. [3] Well on his way, Piccard had to abandon this attempt when China refused him entry, causing him to miss out on needed winds. [4] After weeks of delays trying to get fly-over permission from China, Piccard and British balloonist Brian Jones took off from Chateau d'Oex in the Swiss Alps on March 1, 1999 for a third attempt. [5] They drifted down to North Africa, crossed toward Asia and, with prior permission, passed over southern China. [6] A jet stream whisked them over the Pacific Ocean and then grinded to a near halt over Central America. [7] They picked up another jet stream, and on March 19, beat the endurance record set just two weeks before. [8] On the morning of March 21, after 19 days, 21 hours and 47 minutes aloft, balloonists Piccard and Jones landed on the Saharan sands of southern Egypt. [9] Unfavorable winds prevented their hoped-for landing near the Pyramids. [10] They had crossed their 9 degrees longitude finish line the day before, setting a record for the first nonstop hot-air balloon circumnavigation of the globe. [11] For their feat, Anheuser-Busch Corporation awarded the balloonists a cup and $1 million prize money, some of which they will use to start the Winds of Hope Foundation charity. [12] The International Olympic Committee will award them the Olympic Order.
