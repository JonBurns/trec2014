[1] Bertrand Piccard from Switzerland wanted to be the first to circumnavigate the world non-stop in a balloon. [2] He made three attempts, taking off from a mountain in the Swiss Alps near Lake Geneva with different crewmembers. [3] On his first attempt the balloon landed in the Mediterranean some six hours after take-off because of a fuel leak. [4] On the second attempt he landed in Myanmar when China refused to allow entrance into Chinese air space. [5] The third attempt started on March 1. [6] Piccard and British teammate Brian Jones gained valuable time by obtaining flight permission from China before taking off. [7] They spent six days crossing the Pacific, then flew over Belize and into the Caribbean. [8] The pilots were worried that they might not have enough fuel to reach the finish line, but found favorable winds to carry them across the Atlantic. [9] They spent a final night flying across Africa before landing in the Sahara some 300 miles south of Cairo. [10] They had spent almost three weeks in the balloon and had traveled almost 30,000 miles, then had to wait seven hours for an Egyptian helicopter to fetch them. [11] The International Olympic Committee was to award the Olympic Order to the two balloonists and the balloon was to be permanently housed at the Smithsonian's Air and Space Museum. [12] The two men also received a $1 million prize from the Anheuser-Busch Corporation, half of which was to be donated to charity.
