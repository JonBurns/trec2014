[1] Mark Barton was a loner in high school who dabbled in drugs. [2] He used chemicals to extract a hallucinogen from morning glory seeds, overdosed and went to the emergency room. [3] At age 14 he was arrested for breaking into a drugstore. [4] At 20, he did it again and got off with counseling. [5] After graduating from the U of SC with a degree in chemistry, he went to work in Texas and became president of TLC Manufacturing. [6] After being fired in September 1990, he broke into the building and stole financial records. [7] He was charged with felony burglary but released when the company declined to prosecute. [8] He moved to Georgia with his wife and 2 children, bounced from job to job and began an affair with newlywed Leigh Ann Lang, 21. [9] In August 1999, his wife and mother-in-law were found hacked to death in a camper in Alabama. [10] Mark was the prime suspect in the killing but was never charged for lack of evidence. [11] He collected a large sum from an insurance policy and later married Leigh Ann. [12] In October 1998, he killed the family cat, buried it, then spent two days helping his 8-year-old daughter search for it. [13] In Early 1999, he quit his chemical job and began day-trading, losing as much as $450,000 gambling on internet stocks. [14] In July, Mark Barton, 44, killed his second wife and two children with a hammer, killed nine people and wounded 12 others at two day-trading firms in Atlanta, then committed suicide.
