[1] Forty-four-year-old day trader and chemist Mark Barton went on a shooting rampage at two Atlanta brokerage offices, killing nine people and wounding 13. [2] He eluded police for five hours after the shootings before killing himself as they closed in. [3] Police later discovered the bludgeoned bodies of his estranged wife and two children in her suburban Stockbridge apartment. [4] In a note left near his family's bodies, Barton vowed to kill "the people that greedily sought my destruction". [5] He wrote that he regretted killing his wife, and that he killed his children from a previous marriage, Matthew, 11, and Mychelle, 8, to spare them future pain. [6] His wife was killed two days before the shooting rampage, his children the day before. [7] Investigators pieced together a picture of a distraught man who'd been a loner as a teenager, had paranoid tendencies and a quick temper, and was troubled by unexplained fears. [8] Barton had lost as much as $450,000 on gambling and Internet stock trading in the past year, including $153,000 in a 3-day binge, and $105,000 in the 15-day period before the killing started. [9] Also, Barton was the only suspect in the 1993 bludgeoning deaths of his first wife and her mother, although he was never charged. [10] The tragedies prompted close public scrutiny of the day trading industry's policies for disclosing the risks. [11] Victims and their relatives have filed ten lawsuits in the year following the incident, naming as defendants the two brokerages, their landlords, their security firms and Barton's estate.
