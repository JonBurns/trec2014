[1] The single-engine Piper Saratoga carrying John F. Kennedy, Jr., his wife Carolyn Bessette, and her sister Lauren Bessette crashed on July 16, 1999 into the ocean off Martha's Vineyard. [2] Kennedy and his passenger were flying to Martha's Vineyard from Fairfield, New Jersey, to attend the wedding of his cousin Rory Kennedy's wedding in Hyannis Port. [3] After the plane was overdue, the Kennedy family contacted the Federal Aviation Authority. [4] The US Coast Guard with help from the Air National Guard then began a massive search-and-rescue mission, searching waters along the presumed flight path. [5] The Kennedy family meanwhile maintained a vigil at the Kennedy family compound in Hyannis Port. [6] Forty-eight hours after the crash, federal officials ended the search for survivors and began a search-and-recovery mission. [7] Pieces of the plane washed ashore on Martha's Vineyard and then plane wreckage was found off the coast. [8] Navy divers found the bodies of Kennedy and his passengers entombed in the shattered fuselage of his plane on the ocean floor. [9] Senator Edward Kennedy and his sons Edward Kennedy, Jr., and Patrick Kennedy were then taken to a Navy salvage ship to witness the recovery of the bodies by Navy divers. [10] Senator Kennedy accompanied the bodies to the Coast Guard station at Woods Hole. [11] Kennedy family members decided to bury the ashes of the three at sea in a Navy ceremony. [12] They also decided to have a memorial service at St. Thomas More Church in New York, the church that Kennedy's mother, Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis, often attended.
