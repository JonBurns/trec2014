[1] John F. Kennedy, Jr., was the pilot of a small plane that went missing the night of Friday, July 16, 1999. [2] On board were his wife, Carolyn Bassette Kennedy, and her sister, Lauren Bassette. [3] Although an employee at Martha's Vineyard Airport had reported that the plane was unaccounted for, no action was taken until the Kennedy family contacted the Federal Aviation Administration after midnight. [4] Kennedy was on his way to Martha's Vineyard to drop off his sister-in-law before flying on the Hyannis Port where he and his wife were to attend his cousin's wedding. [5] The wedding was postponed. [6] The Kennedy family gathered at the family compound in Hyannis Port to pray and await word from the massive search. [7] Mass was held for the family. [8] After the search was called off on Sunday, both the Kennedy family and the parents of Carolyn and Lauren Bassette released statements. [9] Sen. Edward Kennedy went to Bridgehampton, N.Y., to meet with and console John's sister, Caroline Kennedy Schlossberg. [10] The wreckage and the bodies were found on Wednesday. [11] Edward Kennedy and his two sons were taken by helicopter to the ship to witness the recovery. [12] Sen. Kennedy then accompanied the bodies to the Coast Guard station. [13] The ashes of all three victims were buried at sea in a closely guarded ceremony aboard a Navy destroyer. [14] The funeral party returned to Hyannis Port for a private reception. [15] A private memorial mass was held at St. Thomas More Church in New York on Friday, July 23.
