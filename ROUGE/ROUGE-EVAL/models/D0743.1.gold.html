[1] A 7.4 Richter earthquake hit Izmit Turkey on August 17, 1999. [2] Richter 5 and 4 aftershocks followed soon after. [3] Nineteen countries sent over 1,000 rescuers. [4] Turkey's rescue efforts were ineffectual. [5] Initial estimates were: 12,018 dead, 45,000 missing, 10,000 trapped, 33,515 under medical treatment beyond thousands treated earlier. [6] Worst affected were Izmit: 4,179 dead, 10,759 injured; Adapazari 3,046 dead, 6,884 injured; and Yalova 2,400 dead, 5,559 injured. [7] Economic loss was $20 billion, including the Izmit Oil Refinery fire, pollution, gas and oil shortages, and power and communications destruction. [8] The Golcuk naval base was destroyed. [9] The August 17 earthquake was two consecutive quakes 20 seconds apart, together lasting 45 seconds, both near Izmit. [10] Another 5 Richter aftershock occurred on August 22 with no serious damage. [11] On August 24 Haymana suffered 4.7 and 4.2 Richter quakes. [12] The refinery fire was controlled. [13] By August 25 foreign rescue teams were leaving. [14] Izmit's deaths grew to 5,179 with 14,718 injuries. [15] Over 200,000 displaced persons needed food, water, shelter and medical facilities. [16] The Netherlands sent 30,000 earthquake- and winter-worthy shelters. [17] The US sent 3,500 all-weather tents. [18] Foreign volunteers brought food, water, and clothing. [19] Local factories gave workers housing. [20] Japan sent prefabricated housing. [21] Richter 5.4 and 4.7 earthquakes struck Izmit on August 31, 1999, hospitalizing 166. [22] On September 13 seven died and hundreds were injured by a 5.8 Richter quake near Izmit. [23] Buildings collapsed in Izmit, Adapazari and Golcuk. [24] Those aftershocks were very disruptive to recovery efforts. [25] By August 2000, 26,000 were still in tents. [26] Deaths reached 17,000.
