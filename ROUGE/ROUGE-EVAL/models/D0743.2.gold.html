[1] Two consecutive earthquakes struck Western Turkey on 17 August 1999. [2] The two quakes registered about 7 on the Richter scale and each lasted about 45 seconds. [3] At least twenty-seven aftershocks followed the quakes. [4] The epicenter was near Izmit, some 70 kilometers southeast of Istanbul. [5] An estimated 13,000 died, 16,000 were seriously injured, more than 100,000 are homeless, and 35,000 are believed to be buried in the wreckage. [6] The damage, totaling billions of dollars, was very extensive to residential and industrial buildings. [7] Coastal naval bases and seaports were destroyed, electric wires were down, water lines were cut, roads cracked, and bridges collapsed. [8] A major fire broke out in the Izmit Refinery. [9] Rescue teams with supplies and equipment arrived from US bases in Turkey and from all over the world. [10] Two American search-rescue teams and 1,000 relief workers from nineteen countries scoured crumbling buildings for trapped survivors. [11] Firefighters from eight European countries succeeded in controlling the refinery fires. [12] Nearly every European country sent money. [13] Relief workers and Turkey's Psychological Association are counseling children. [14] Hospitals are flooded with the injured and some 200,000 homeless are living in tents, educational facilities, and in open area. [15] There is an overwhelming need for portable toilets, garbage collection, disposal of bodies, and reconstruction of living facilities and factories. [16] Estimated cost of recovery is between $4.5 and $17 million. [17] Although severely criticized for its failures, the government is confident of recovery because of a construction boom, extensive measures to cope with new quakes, and improvement of construction standards.
